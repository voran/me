Bio

I was born in Sofia, Bulgaria on 15 September 1989. This makes me exactly old. I was born into a family of scientists, and was raised to love science in all its forms. I also developed an early interest in music, and started playing the piano at the age of six. Nowadays I mostly play guitar and clarinet. I also enjoy travelling and outdoor photography. I love hiking, camping, skiing, and swimming. I have travelled to many places in the United States and Europe, and I will travel to many more whenever I get the chance.

Education

I hold a Bachelor of Arts degree in Computer Science and Economics with a minor in Math from the American University in Bulgaria. I also spent one semester in the Otto-von-Guericke University in Magdeburg, Germany as an Erasmus student. During my senior year, I received the Bulgarian Young Leaders Award for 2011 and spent three academic quarters at the Rochester Institute of Technology. I attended the First English Language School and graduated with honors. I also attended the National School of Music where I received several awards for my piano playing.

Interests

My interests in Computer Science are in the areas of Operating Systems, Algorithms, and Distributed Systems. I think P != NP, and I'll live to see it proven. I like Java, but I also like the low-level stuff: C, Assembly. I run Arch Linux on all my devices, except my phone. It runs MeeGo.

My interests in Economics are mainly in the field of Economic Policy. I believe that the economy needs some regulation, because competition is *not* perfect in the real world. There is no perfect information. Most markets "suffer" from economies of scale.
